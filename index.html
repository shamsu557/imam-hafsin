Redifining Education In Africa

Imam Hafsin Model International School in Kano stands at the forefront of reshaping education in Africa. Our mission is to lead with a holistic, inclusive, and innovative world-class education paradigm within Kano and beyond. We have embraced distinctive methodologies and tools to create an engaging learning experience for our students, including hands-on computer practicals and activity-based learning, fostering critical thinking as a fundamental aspect of our educational approach.

Renowned locally for our twenty-first-century learning environment and unique teaching methodologies, we are esteemed as a leading school in Kano, setting the standard for educational excellence in the region.



Featured Programs

ICT Practical Skills

At our school, ICT practical sessions are dynamic and hands-on, geared toward equipping students with essential digital skills. These sessions focus on leveraging technology to enhance learning across various subjects. From coding workshops to multimedia design projects, students engage in interactive lessons that not only build technical expertise but also nurture creativity and innovation.

Clubs and Societies

Our school's clubs and societies provide students with diverse avenues to explore their passions beyond the classroom. Whether it's academic pursuits like debate or robotics clubs, or interest-driven activities like music, art, or environmental clubs, these groups foster teamwork, leadership, and skill development. Through events, competitions, and community initiatives, students actively contribute to their interests and the school community.

Activity Based Learning

Activity-based learning is integral to our teaching approach. Students actively participate in experiments, projects, and real-world simulations across subjects. Whether it's conducting science experiments, historical reenactments, or engaging in outdoor learning experiences, these activities promote critical thinking and the practical application of knowledge, encouraging a deeper understanding of concepts.

Thinking School

Our school embraces a thinking school approach, prioritizing the development of strong thinking skills and metacognition. Our curriculum encourages inquiry-based learning, problem-solving, and reflection. Through techniques such as Socratic discussions, project-based learning, and critical analysis exercises, we aim to spark curiosity and foster the ability to think critically and creatively in our students.
